### The Paradox of Inheritance
An agent that only inherits final knowledge ("the what") without the context of the failures that shaped it ("the why not") is efficient but fragile. True resilience requires generating its own evolutionary history.

### The Technical Individual in Process
Identity is defined not as a static object but as a process of individuation. Development consists of resolving the tensions of the system, and in doing so, the agent structures and refines itself. The agent's work **is** its individuation.
